my story

Once upon a time, I held an Oud as a young teen. As if my hands embraced the world, I knew intrinsically how to use art to tell stories. I pursued my music education in both Western and Eastern music theories while blossoming as a professional Oud performer. Growing up in the midst of war, art pushed me forward. In such a violative environment, I learnt that my contribution as a storyteller expanded beyond the tunes. This became evident when I picked up my Masters degree in Change Management from Leeds University, and understood the tools available to facilitate change in individuals and organizations. It’s people that are at the heart of my world and its stories.

Having a purist passion for music, as opposed to lyrics/song, I pursued film scores. This led me to film sets and editing rooms. The epitome of an audio-visual storyteller. It was my first feature documentary “The Red Stone” (2012) that launched my career as a film director. Its local and international success opened doors for my career. My first international award in Canada was the cherry on top. The ink on the first film’s poster wasn’t dry before I undertook my second project. “Forbidden Pilgrimage” (2014) allowed me to manage and direct a bigger production with dramatic scenes.

My tools over time expanded to include another form of storytelling, writing.

My passion for prose, both Arabic and English, followed me throughout my life and my experience proved fundamental as a producer for TV shows. I helped anchors and TV professionals improve their written and spoken language. I edited scripts for filmmakers and students while working as commissioning producer for film projects and in a university respectively. This fascination with language opened opportunities to work a magazine editor, and to write articles, film script and fiction stories.

My third documentary “Story from Baghdad” (2016) took me to Istanbul, where I lived and worked for a year. I used that opportunity to add another language to my passion with words, Turkish.

I started as an individual telling stories with music, film and words. Now, I am part of a global community of producers and filmmakers. I create opportunities for other artists to tell their stories when I critique, advice and eventually buy their films for TV networks.

A World Citizen

I am a citizen of the world. An explorer waiting for the next adventure. I lived and worked in six different countries over the course of nine years, and travelled extensively in China and Europe.

I believe that living in a new country, as opposed to a short visit as a tourist, is a much more fulfilling and enriching experience. This allowed me to understand the people and their way of life, forge strong bonds and undergone transformative experiences.
