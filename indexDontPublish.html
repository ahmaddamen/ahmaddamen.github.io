my story

After graduating with an MSc in Change Management from Leeds University, I left my job in Marketing to pursue film and music. Eventually, the business graduate ended up with 10 years of experience in the film and TV industry, 3 feature films, and film music scores for almost 20 feature documentaries, independent fiction films and other TV projects.

My first feature documentary “The Red Stone” (2012) was an international success and launched my career in film, and gave me my first film award. “Forbidden Pilgrimage” (2014) was officially selected in festivals in London, Bristol, Qatar, Morocco and Canada. It was used in European legal campaigns for fair tourism practices.

I enjoyed my work in Birzeit University as a TV unit supervisor, working closely with students and professionals to hone their skills. In an effort to expand my expertise, I moved to Istanbul for a year. I worked there on my 3rd feature film A Story from Baghdad, and was part of a Masters music project at the ITU University Istanbul Conservatory of Music.

I now live in Virginia, USA on a permanent basis to work in film acquisitions, signing deals with major American and International networks.

A World Citizen

I am a citizen of the world. An explorer waiting for the next adventure. I lived and worked in six different countries over the course of nine years, and travelled extensively in China and Europe.

I believe that living in a new country, as opposed to a short visit as a tourist, is a much more fulfilling and enriching experience. This allowed me to understand the people and their way of life, forge strong bonds and undergone transformative experiences.
