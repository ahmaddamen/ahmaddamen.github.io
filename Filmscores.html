Music Background and influence

My journey with music started with Oud lessons and Eastern music theory in the age of 11. Within few years I found myself drawn to composing music. I wrote my first short piece at the age of 14. During my MSc studies in England, I acquired my ABRSM certificate in Western music theory with Distinction. I travelled to Jordan and trained under Maestro Mohammad Ottoman Siddiq in Jordan and other teachers.

I continued to develop my Oud skills and recorded for several Middle Eastern composers in Jordan and Palestine. During his time in England and Turkey, I played with several bands dedicated to combining Middle Eastern music with other World Music instruments. This helped me develop my own improvisational style which adopts swing jazz into World Music traditions having worked and performed with Indian, Middle African and Western instruments. The film composers i look up to and continue to learn from include Maurice Jarre, Ennio Morricone, Hans Zimmer, Tchaikovsky, Beethoven, Amon Tobin, Nino Rota and many others.

I secured an opportunity to pursue a Master’s degree in music at the Turkish music conservatory at the Istanbul Technical University, but I left the program unfinished after I got an offer to work in the United States.

Film Projects

I composed my first soundtrack for a feature film in 2010 and since then I composed soundtracks for more than 15 feature documentaries and other short fiction and TV projects. They include Sneaking (2010), Land Owners series (2010), Trees without Roots (2012), Price of Oslo (2013) and Switzerland – The Armed People (2015) amongst several others. In addition, I composed the music for the documentaries that I directed “The Red Stone” (2012) and “Forbidden Pilgrimage” (2014).
